Introduction to Self-Driving Cars

What is Your Target Technology?

Self-driving cars, also known as autonomous vehicles, use advanced technologies such as artificial intelligence, sensors, and machine learning to navigate roads without human intervention. They represent a significant step forward in transportation innovation.

Why Are They Important?

Autonomous vehicles aim to improve road safety, reduce traffic congestion, and provide mobility for those unable to drive. Statistics suggest a potential 90% reduction in accidents caused by human error, saving thousands of lives annually.

Additionally, self-driving cars could lead to smarter urban planning and lower greenhouse gas emissions by optimizing traffic flow and reducing unnecessary trips.

Self-Driving Cars Application Domain

The integration of self-driving cars into society raises questions about their impact on the job market, urban planning, and environmental sustainability. Delivery services, ride-sharing platforms, and even emergency response vehicles are potential beneficiaries.

However, the technology faces challenges in ethical decision-making, regulatory approval, and public trust. These factors require thorough exploration for successful adoption.
